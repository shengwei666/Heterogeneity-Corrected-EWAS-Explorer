Introduction

Genome-wide DNA methylation arrays have powered large-scale epigenome-wide association studies (EWAS), yet conventional quantification based on averaged methylation levels often collapses critical cell-to-cell variability, blunting statistical power and biological interpretability—especially in complex, heterogeneous tissues. To overcome this limitation, we develop a principled data transformation that maps array-derived average methylation measurements into two heterogeneity-aware metrics—CHALM and CAMDA—to sharpen effect estimates and reveal disease-relevant epigenetic patterns with higher fidelity.

CHALM (Cell Heterogeneity-Adjusted cLonal Methylation; Xu et al., Nat Commun, 2021) quantifies the promoter methylation as the ratio of methylated reads (with ≥1 mCpG) to total reads mapped to a given promoter region. CAMDA (Concurrence of Active Methylation and De-methylAtion; Shi et al., Nat Commun, 2021) quantifies the concurrence of active DNA methylation and demethylation within the same cell by dissecting reads from bisulfite sequencing.

When propagated through EWAS meta-analyses, CHALM and CAMDA consistently enhance detection of disease-associated CpGs—including subtle yet functionally meaningful signals—while reducing noise and confounding from tissue composition. This framework opens a path for large-scale, heterogeneity-corrected EWAS that advances biomarker discovery, clarifies the causal axes of methylation dynamics in disease onset and progression, and strengthens precision medicine by improving early diagnosis, prognosis stratification, and target nomination.

This website hosts curated summaries across phenotypes, tissues, and analytical types (Continuous, Binary, Survival), with harmonized effect estimates under Beta, CHALM, and CAMDA.
